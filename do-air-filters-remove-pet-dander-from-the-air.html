Do Air Filters Remove Pet Dander from The Air?

Air filters are essential in every home. They clean the air of harmful substances and microorganisms, keeping your family healthy. Unfortunately, air filters cannot remove pet dander, which is one of the most common indoor allergens. Luckily, there are several ways pet owners can reduce pet dander in their homes. In addition to keeping your pet out of certain rooms, there are things you can do to reduce pet dander indoors. The good news is that these solutions wonâ€™t break the bank and are easy to implement. Whether you have a fur baby or a friend who brings furry friends over, read on for helpful tips on how to reduce pet dander in your home.

Change your bedding frequently

Pet dander that settles on fabrics can be re-released into the air when you change your bedding. To reduce pet dander, you can change your bedding more frequently. Dust mites, which are another common indoor allergen, thrive in moist, dark environments. By changing your bedding more frequently, you can reduce the amount of time dust mites have to thrive in your home. Another option is to replace your bedding with allergen-resistant fabrics. Many fabrics are treated with stain-resistant agents and can reduce pet dander, dust mites, and other allergens.

Air purifiers

Air purifiers with HEPA and carbon filters are effective at reducing pet dander. However, they are not necessary for every home. If you have pets, you should still have a good quality air filter in your HVAC system. An air purifier can help reduce pet dander in areas where there is little air flow, like in the corners of rooms or near heating and cooling vents.An air purifier can be a good investment if you have pets or live in a high-pet-dander area. If you have allergies and want to reduce pet dander, an air purifier can help.

Keep your pet out of the bedroom

Pet dander is most commonly found in the bedrooms. This is because most people sleep, which means there is less air flow. Unfortunately, this also means pet dander is more likely to settle in the bedrooms. To reduce the amount of pet dander in your bedroom, keep your pet out of the room. This can be challenging, especially if you have multiple pets.You can also reduce pet dander in your bedroom by reducing the amount of time you spend in the room. If you spend less time in the bedroom, there will be less time for pet dander to settle.

Use a vacuum cleaner with a specialized filter

Some vacuum cleaners come with a specialized filter that is designed to reduce pet dander. These filters trap pet hair and other particles, making them easier to clean and reducing the amount of pet dander in your home. Some pet owners even use these filters in their car to reduce pet dander when bringing their pet to the veterinarian.

Conclusion

Pet owners know how difficult it can be to keep pets away from their furniture, rugs, and other items in the home. Pet dander can be found in many places, including bedding, upholstery, and rugs. Luckily, there are several ways pet owners can reduce pet dander in their homes. By keeping your pet out of certain rooms and using an air purifier with a carbon filter, you can reduce pet dander.

Frequently Asked Question

Does having the right air filter make a difference
Having the right air filter makes a difference in cleaning your indoor air and keeping your air quality safe. It removes airborne pollutants which may harm your health. It prevents you from getting allergies.
How often do you change your air filters in a year
Typically, if you have a tiny healthy home, it would take you every 6-12 months to replace your filter. Commonly for families of standard sizes or more, it is recommended that you change your filter every 3 months or 90 days. If you have a cat or dog, it is better if you replace it more frequently, like every 2 months. If you have more than one, then you better replace it every 20-45 days.
